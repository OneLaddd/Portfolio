Chapter One

About Me

While I was quite studious, I wasn't the person to dream big. At high school, I was someone who often achieve good honors but that would be it. I never dreamed of becoming a computer engineer nor any careers, really.

I was just living.

One day, we visited one of my older cousins. He was a software engineer and seeing him program looked cool. It's quite interesting because, despite my parents pushing myself to be a civil engineer for years, this 1-hour interaction would one day decide my career as a Computer Engineer (hopefully).

It was a case of a butterfly effect.

FreeCodeCamp was the first online course I took, as suggested by my older cousin. It was great, but it didn't really teach me much since I was just following instructions. CS50x, on the other hand, provided more freedom but was very challenging—it took me three weeks to solve the hardest problem set.

I've learned how to program, but I also learned what grit is.

For some time, I was very interested in front-end web Development. Even before I started coding, I was passionate about creating art. While websites are all about straightforwardness, there is something pleasing when it also tells a story.

<h5>
    Logic is sometimes artistic.
</h5>

Later on, front-end web development tired me out and made me want to try new things. In our final year of Senior High School, we were tasked with conducting research, and foolishly committed to the herculean task of creating a tomato sorter without understanding its implications. The sacrifice required sleepless nights and meant staring at VS Code even on New Year's Eve.

Fortunately, we came through,

...lowkey getting the Best Research Award in the whole school and a ticket to a competition in DLSU.

So, does this mean I wanted to be a computer engineer? Personally, I would still prefer computer science, but my parents were desperate for me to have the title "engineer" in any career I pursued, leaving me with no choice but to follow that path. At some point, however, I began to think that maybe it was the right choice after all, given my growing interest in Artificial Intelligence and Machines.

Hence, I'm here now.

Chapter Four

Personal Journey

The Start

Prior to entering Mapua University, I have an impression that I would get no friends at all. Not because I am bad at making one, but because I thought everybody else is very studious and goal-focused. Though, this was proven wrong even at the very opening ceremony. I have met two people there from different courses and are open to have conversation with me. Apparently, most people there are similar to me, they are not that different. Oh by the way, the ceremony was the coolest thing I've seen!

The First Class

It was still the opening week but one of my professors opened an online class for our block. It was a GED class and I really had quite a fearful expression because he not only made a performance task for us, but also kicked someone out the zoom call for not answering the question about his rules properly. I thought that every class would be like this. However later on, it isn't that bad at it seems. Also, the GED professor I'm talking about is actually the best professor I've had in Mapua so far due to his strict but very humorous and engaging lectures.

Meeting Friends
Part One

In a class, I've met two of my friend because we were bad-mouthing the previously said GED professor cause he was strict (but more than that, bald.) Later on meeting and immediately deepening our relationship with the other four classmates I've had because of a racist joke. Pronouncing his surname awfully sounds like an black-african slur that most may be familiar with. And yes, I'm not really a good person.

About Exams

There was a huge leap of difficulty when we had our first quiz, especially in Trigonometry. I was scared because I may not pass, and prior to that, we have our quiz for RPH, getting a measly score of 3/10. Later on, the Trigonometry Prof announced that there are only two that have passed the quiz from our section and I was overjoyed for being one of them.

Homeworks

ALEKS, Cengage, Wiley, MATLAB, Coursera... There's a lot of them especially in Math Courses. But regardless, I pushed through, this is nothing in comparison to the sleepless days I've spent with my practical research 2. The trick is to gaslight myself to enjoying the process to eventually enjoy the course genuinely. Even at the second term, there's a ton of these tasks, but is still manageable.

About Professors
Part One

All the teachers in my course are to be appreciated however, there are some odd encounters I've experience with them. In my algebra class, I was able to strengthen my courage to stand up and participate in the board. But, I actually get roasted by the prof, calling my behavior "grade school" with me writing 1=x instead of x=1 as my final answer. There is also that one case wherein the whole entire period, one professor rants about the wrongness of questioning a teacher's credibility and integrity instead of tackling the lesson.

1st Term Ends

I was happy to pass the 1st term and actually getting into the Dean's List. But, most of my friends are not so fortunate, failing a class or two. Therefore, although we still see each other, the instance is very rare. Though this is a testament that Mapua University is hard.

Meeting Friends
Part Two

Since most of my friends failed a class, we were not able to be at the same section. At the second term, I met four of my classmates before that I was not able to befriend early on. We were eventually becoming more friendly later on, talking about odd professors and engage in groupworks.

About Professors
Part Two

Like in the first term, some professors caught my attention. My linear algebra professor teaches so fast that he rarely uses half the scheduled time. I initially disliked this but grew to appreciate it since I could ask questions, and he explained well before ending early. Another professor spends two hours sharing random stories, like a student getting murdered, without covering the full lesson. Lastly, a muscular computer engineering graduate shattered my expectation that people in computer-related fields are either skinny or fat.

About STS

Science, Technology, & Society was so challenging that it deserves its own section, though not for the best reasons. I expected major subjects like calculus to be time-consuming, but this was even harder. It led me to create my Multi-Hazard Detection System prototype, as we had to develop a unique project and pitch it to a fundable institution. Despite the difficulty, I appreciate the professor, and it's not her fault. I still gained something valuable—the judge liked my prototype enough to suggest turning it into a startup.

This Portfolio

Another subject that took away a lot of my time is CPE 101. However, this would be my fault because I've made a decision to make the website out of code instead of using website-creation platforms like Wix. It has been a year since I've made a website, so I thought that I should recall all the learnings I have to this one big project. And yes, I kinda regret it now (but also appreciate it).

About the Present

Hopefully, I pass all subjects from the 2nd term. Some quizzes are relatively easy but some are hard enough to stumble even the smartest ones from the class. More often, I I would be very thankful that there are additional points added to my quizzes.
